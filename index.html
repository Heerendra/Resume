Project Description

AOS (Airport Operating System) plays a crucial role in managing various operational aspects within airports, including stand allocation, desk allocation, gate management, and baggage handling.

Stand Allocation: AOS optimizes the allocation of aircraft stands, ensuring efficient use of available space and resources. It considers factors such as aircraft size, arrival/departure schedules, and maintenance requirements to allocate appropriate stands for parking and servicing aircraft.

Desk Allocation: AOS streamlines the allocation of check-in desks and counters based on passenger traffic, flight schedules, and airline requirements. It ensures that each airline has the necessary space and resources to manage passenger check-in processes smoothly.

Gate Management: AOS facilitates effective gate management by coordinating the arrival and departure of flights at designated gates. It considers factors such as aircraft type, departure/arrival times, and boarding processes to optimize gate utilization and minimize delays.

Baggage Handling: AOS plays a critical role in managing baggage handling processes, including baggage check-in, sorting, tracking, and loading onto aircraft. It ensures that baggage is efficiently transferred between terminals, flights, and connecting flights, minimizing the risk of mishandling and delays.

Overall, AOS enhances operational efficiency and passenger experience by optimizing resource allocation, streamlining processes, and minimizing disruptions in stand allocation, desk allocation, gate management, and baggage handling within airports.
